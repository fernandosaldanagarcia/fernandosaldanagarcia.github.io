Fernando Saldaña

Postdoctoral Researcher in Mathematical Epidemiology

Profile

I’m an applied mathematician currently working on the mathematical modeling of infectious diseases. In general, I’m interested in using mathematical modeling and simulation methods to problems in biology, ecology, and public health. I have significant experience in the analysis and formulation of Kermack-McKendrick-type models, stochastic modeling, Python programming, optimal control theory, machine learning, statistical methods, and parameter inference.

I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at the INRAE (French National Research Institute for Agriculture, Food and Environment), within the BIOEPAR team.
